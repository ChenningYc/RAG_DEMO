RAG stands for Retrieval-Augmented Generation.

A RAG system combines information retrieval with text generation.

First, documents are collected from sources such as PDFs, websites, databases, or internal knowledge bases.

Next, the documents are cleaned and split into smaller chunks.

Each chunk is converted into a searchable representation, often using embeddings.

When the user asks a question, the system searches for the most relevant chunks.

The retrieved chunks are placed into the prompt as context.

The language model then answers using the provided context.

RAG is useful when the answer depends on private, updated, or domain-specific information that may not exist in the language model's training data.
